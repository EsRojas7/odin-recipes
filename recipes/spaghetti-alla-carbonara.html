Spaghetti alla carbonara

Credit to Dotdash Meredith Food Studios for all images used

Description

Carbonara is a Roman dish made with eggs, hard cheese, and cured pork.

It's signature rich and silky sauce comes from beaten eggs tossed with hot pasta.

The trick to making carbonara is making sure the pasta is hot enought to cook the eggs,

but not so hot that they curdle.

Ingredients to Note:

  • Olive Oil: Use 2 teaspoons Olive oil suitable for heat.
  • Guanciale: Buy 1 pound Guanciale, or unsmoked bacon/pancetta if you can't find Guanciale.
  • Spaghetti: 16oz store bought or homemade. Even better if you find Bucatini.
  • Eggs: 3 large eggs to add richness and flavor.
  • Cheese: 10 tablespoons Pecorino romano and grate it yourself. But Parmigiano-Reggiano could also be used.
  • Seasoning: Just salt and freshly ground black pepper.

Process of elaboration

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet on medium heat; add guanciale.
    2. Cook, turning occasionally, until evenly browned and crispy, for 5 to 10 minutes. Once cooked remove from heat and drain on paper towels.

  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water
    3. stirring occasionally until tender yet firm to the bite, about 9 minutes. Drain and return to the pot.
